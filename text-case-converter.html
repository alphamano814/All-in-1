Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between Title Case and Sentence Case?
Title Case capitalizes the first letter of every word, while Sentence Case only capitalizes the first letter of each sentence. Title Case is typically used for titles and headings, while Sentence Case is used for regular paragraphs and body text.
What is CamelCase used for?
CamelCase (also known as camel case) is commonly used in programming for variable names, function names, and other identifiers. It removes spaces between words and capitalizes each word except the first one (e.g., "camelCaseExample").
Can I convert large amounts of text?
Yes, our Text Case Converter can handle large amounts of text efficiently. However, for extremely large documents, we recommend converting sections at a time for optimal performance.

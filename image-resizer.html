How to Use the Image Resizer

  1. Upload your images by dragging and dropping them into the upload area or clicking "Choose Files"
  2. Select your preferred resize mode - Dimensions, Percentage, Max Dimension, or Preset
  3. Adjust the settings according to your needs:
    • Dimensions: Set specific width and height values
    • Percentage: Scale images by percentage (1-500%)
    • Max Dimension: Set the maximum width or height
    • Preset: Choose from popular social media and display sizes
  4. Choose output format and quality settings for optimal file size and quality balance
  5. Click "Resize All Images" to process your images
  6. Download individual images or use "Download All as ZIP" for batch download

Supported Formats

  • Output formats: Original format, JPEG, PNG, WebP

Frequently Asked Questions

Is there a limit to the number of images I can resize?
No, there are no limits. You can resize as many images as you want, with no restrictions on usage.
What is the maximum file size for resizing?
The tool can handle images up to 10MB in size. For best performance with larger files, we recommend resizing them to smaller dimensions first.
Are my images stored on your servers?
No, all image processing happens directly in your browser. Your images never leave your device, ensuring complete privacy.
Which resize mode should I use?
  • Dimensions: When you need specific width and height values
  • Percentage: When you want to scale images proportionally (e.g., reduce to 50%)
  • Max Dimension: When you want to ensure images don't exceed a certain size
  • Preset: For social media platforms and standard display sizes
What's the difference between maintaining aspect ratio and cropping?
Maintaining aspect ratio keeps the original proportions of your image while resizing. Cropping trims the image to fit exact dimensions, which may remove parts of the image.
